enum Lock_Type

Gen2 protocol tag lock types.

Different results based on the locked area. Unlocking is the reverse operation of locking, but permanent locking is irreversible. Locking can be considered as access restriction or access protection to the Gen2 storage area.Unlocking refers to the operation of removing the password lock. Unlocking is the reverse of locking, but permanent locking operations are irreversible.

  • After locking the password area, it becomes unreadable and unwritable without the password.
  • EPC and USER areas are readable but not writable.Writing requires a password without unlocking.
  • The TID area is generally permanently locked at the factory; permanent locking means it can only be read, even with the password, and cannot be written or unlocked.
  • If permanently locked, the password will also be invalid. The reserved area is not readable or writable, and other areas are not writable.
  • If the area is permanently unlocked,it can't lock again.
